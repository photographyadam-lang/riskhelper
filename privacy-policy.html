Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 January 2025 · Last updated: February 2025

We collect server access logs automatically and use a privacy-friendly, cookie-less analytics tool (Umami) to understand total site visits. If you contact us by email, we store that correspondence. We do not use advertising trackers and we will not sell your data. Full details below.

1. Who we are

RiskHelper.ai is a trading name of Risk Helper LLC, a company registered in Delaware. We operate the website at riskhelper.ai and the Level 0 Risk Assessment tool accessible from that site.

For the purposes of the UK GDPR, EU GDPR, and applicable US state privacy laws, we are the data controller for personal data processed in connection with your use of our website.

Contact: For all privacy enquiries, write to contact@riskhelper.ai.

2. What data we collect and why

2.1 Data you give us directly

Email enquiries. When you contact us at contact@riskhelper.ai, we receive and store your email address and the content of your message. We use this to respond to you. We do not add you to a marketing list without your explicit consent.

2.2 Data collected automatically

Server access logs. Our hosting provider (Hostinger) automatically records access logs when you visit our website. These logs typically contain: your IP address, the date and time of the request, the page or resource requested, the HTTP status code returned, your browser type and operating system, and the referring URL. We use these logs for security monitoring, diagnosing technical problems, and understanding aggregate traffic patterns. We do not use IP addresses to identify individual visitors.

Your assessment answers. The Level 0 assessment runs entirely in your browser. Your answers to the assessment questions are stored only in your browser's memory for the duration of your session. They are not transmitted to our servers. When you close the browser tab, your answers are gone. We receive no record of what you answered.

2.3 Analytics

We use Umami, a privacy-respecting analytics tool, to understand website traffic. Umami does not use cookies, does not track you across other websites, and anonymizes your IP address. It only provides us with aggregate, non-personal data about how our website is used.

2.4 Data we do not collect

  • We do not collect payment information — there is no paid product at this time.
  • We do not create user accounts — no registration is required to use the Level 0 tool.
  • We do not use advertising networks or tracking pixels.
  • We do not sell data to any third party for any purpose.

3. Legal bases (GDPR and UK GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process personal data under the following legal bases:

Processing activity Legal basis Notes
Responding to email enquiries Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) Our interest in responding to communications is not overridden by your interests or rights
Server access logs (security and diagnostics) Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) Processing is necessary to protect the security and integrity of our systems
Analytics (Umami) Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) To understand aggregate website usage without identifying or tracking individual users

4. Cookies and tracking

We use cookies as follows:

Essential cookies (no consent required)

  • riskhelper_cookie_consent — stores your cookie consent choice. Expires after 12 months. Without this, we cannot remember that you have seen the cookie banner.

Analytics

We use a privacy-friendly analytics tool called Umami that does not use cookies to track visitors. Therefore, no analytics cookies are set on your device by our analytics service.

Third-party cookies

We load fonts from Google Fonts. Google's servers log requests for fonts, which may include your IP address. This is a connection to Google's servers, not a tracking cookie. See Google's privacy policy for details. We use the font-display: swap technique to minimise this connection where possible.

Managing your cookie preferences

You can change your consent choice at any time by clicking "Cookie settings" in our website footer. You can also delete cookies directly in your browser settings — see your browser's help pages for instructions.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR and UK GDPR, you have the following rights. We will respond to all requests within one calendar month.

  • Right of access (Article 15). You can ask us what personal data we hold about you and receive a copy.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16). You can ask us to correct inaccurate data we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure (Article 17). You can ask us to delete personal data we hold about you where there is no compelling reason to continue processing it.
  • Right to restriction (Article 18). You can ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Article 20). Where processing is based on consent or a contract, you can ask us to provide your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Article 21). You can object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will stop processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.
  • Rights related to automated decision-making. We do not make automated decisions about individuals that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at contact@riskhelper.ai. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request. We will not charge a fee for reasonable requests.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. In the UK, this is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). In the EEA, contact the supervisory authority in your country of residence.

9. California privacy rights (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) gives you additional rights over your personal information.

Your California rights

  • Right to know. You can request details about the personal information we have collected about you and how we have used and disclosed it.
  • Right to delete. You can request that we delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to correct. You can request that we correct inaccurate personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell personal information. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising. No opt-out is required, but you may submit a request to confirm this at any time.
  • Right to non-discrimination. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

As stated above, we do not sell or share personal information for advertising purposes. To confirm this or to submit a data request, email contact@riskhelper.ai with the subject line "California Privacy Request".

10. UK and Canadian residents

United Kingdom

Processing of UK residents' personal data is governed by UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Our legal bases and your rights are as described in Sections 3 and 8 above. To complain to the UK regulator, contact the ICO.

Canada (PIPEDA)

Canadian residents' data is processed in accordance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). We collect, use, and disclose personal information only with your knowledge and consent, or as required by law. You may request access to or correction of your personal information by writing to contact@riskhelper.ai. To complain to the Canadian regulator, contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

11. Children's data

The RiskHelper.ai website and Level 0 assessment are directed at adults — specifically, professional Product Managers and compliance professionals. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13 (or 16 for EU/UK residents). If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child, please contact us immediately at contact@riskhelper.ai and we will delete it promptly.

12. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful loss, alteration, disclosure, or access. These measures include:

  • HTTPS encryption for all data in transit
  • HTTP security headers (Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy)
  • No transmission of assessment answers to our servers — they remain in your browser only
  • Access controls on hosting infrastructure
  • Regular review of third-party processors' security practices

No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of information you send to us by email.

13. Changes to this policy

We will update this policy when our data practices change materially — for example, when we add user accounts, implement analytics, or engage new processors. We will post the updated policy on this page and update the "Last updated" date at the top. For significant changes, we may notify you directly if we hold contact details for you.
