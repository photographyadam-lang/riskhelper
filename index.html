The problem

Compliance finds the problem.
After you've already built it.

"The features that create the most compliance problems aren't the ones teams knew were risky. They're the ones nobody thought to check until we were two weeks away from launch."

Most teams bring compliance in too late. Others don't have access to the right resources early enough to build with all risk areas accounted for. Either way, the result is the same: Last-minute rework, delayed launches, and burned goodwill.

This is a fixable problem. But it requires asking the right questions early before you've starting design and coding.

🕐

The Playground Framework

Every product idea lands somewhere on this map.

There's a concept we developed called the Playground Framework. Named after how a children's playground is structured, with a soft floor, a supervised area, and a fence. Here it's really clear where's safe to play, where you need supervision, and where you need to be extra careful. Most teams don't know which of their ideas can be explored freely, which may need a little guidance to help understand the risks, and which need to bring in legal and compliance early.

The soft floor

Move fast. Likely easy review ahead of launch.

A new filter UI. A settings redesign. A notification preference screen. No meaningful privacy, AI, or safety risk. Your team can build and iterate freely.

Who we are

We've been on both sides
of this conversation.

We are Adam Buteux and Ali Chouhdry, co-founders of Risk Helper. We both ran risk reviews at Meta, assessing risks related to privacy, online safety, AI, the protection of children online.

We saw the same pattern repeat. Teams that came to us for feedback early, on ideas and before products were developed, shipped cleaner products faster. Teams that came to us once the product was built often had to rework features, had delayed launches, and burned goodwill on both sides.

When product teams understood where the risk boundaries were, they didn't slow down, they sped up. FAQ

Common questions.

What is Risk Helper?

A free assessment tool that tells Product Managers which privacy, AI, online safety, and children's risk areas are relevant to their feature — in under 5 minutes. Covers GDPR, CCPA, EU AI Act, COPPA, and related frameworks across US, EU, UK, and Canadian regulatory contexts.

Who is it for?

Primarily Product Managers who want to understand risk earlier in the development cycle — at ideation, not implementation. Also used by compliance and legal teams who want product teams better prepared before reviews, and by companies without a dedicated in-house compliance function.

Is it free?

Level 0 — the risk triage assessment — is free. No credit card, no account required to start. Level 1 and Level 2 are in development; pricing has not yet been announced.

What risk areas does it cover?

Four domains: privacy and data protection, AI and algorithmic risk, online safety and harm prevention, and children's safety. Focuses on US, EU, UK, and Canadian regulatory contexts including GDPR, CCPA, EU AI Act, and COPPA.

Is this legal advice?

No. Risk Helper provides directional guidance for early-stage product decisions. It is not legal advice and is not a substitute for review by a qualified legal or compliance professional. Always have your designs reviewed before launch.

Who built it?

Adam Buteux and Ali Chouhdry, who ran privacy risk reviews together at Meta. Before Meta, Adam built the privacy program at Audible and spent a decade at PwC on regulatory compliance for Fortune 500 companies. CISSP, CIPM, MBA, Computer Science.
